Pros and Cons Globalism

What Positive and Negative Effects does Globalisation Have?

 Globalism comes with both upsides and downsides. Becoming more interdependent on one another can have massive benefits such as a more peaceful world, however becoming more interdependent can cause problems for the individual and local communities. Globalism can lead to more distributed wealth throughout the world and help save people from poverty. According to the president of the World Bank over a billion people have lifted themselves out of extreme poverty and that global poverty is at a lower rate than ever before. Despite helping impoverished people, globalism has ruined local economies by flooding the market with cheap external goods that can be imported cheaper than local companies can produce. The result of the imports is a decreased quality of life because local manufacturers shut down as they are unable to compete with the large transnational corporations leading to a decrease in job opportunities which leaves the citizens poorer. Globalism allows certain things such as the internet to work which improves the quality of life for people with access to technology; for those without access to modern technology it proves no real benefit and instead adds to the gap between rich and poor. For the typical person in a developed country globalisation plays a massive role on the quality of life and from the perspective of someone within a developed country it would seem to be a positive thing. It provides them with companies such as amazon which allows them to ship nearly anything to their doorstep. It would also give them access to foods and products from outside their country. For example, in Canada there are a multitude of American owned businesses like McDonalds which without globalisation would not be able to operate within the country. To summarise, globalisation has a better effect on the quality of life of someone within a developed country compared to a citizen of a developing country.
